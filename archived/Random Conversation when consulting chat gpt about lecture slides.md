This concept is from **Joseph Juran‚Äôs quality philosophy**, specifically his ideas on **self-control** or a **self-managed process** ‚Äî part of his broader **Quality Control** and **Quality Improvement** teachings within the _Juran Trilogy_.

### üîç Explanation

**Self-control** means designing work so each person can control their own performance without constant external supervision. It complements classical control (manager-driven) by focusing on internal accountability.

* * *

### üß© Key Ideas

| **Classical Control** | **Self-Control** |
| --- | --- |
| External management sets standards and monitors results. | Individuals understand and regulate their own performance. |
| Emphasis during execution (reactive). | Emphasis before execution (proactive). |
| Manager measures, compares, and corrects. | Worker knows expectations, performance, and corrective actions. |

* * *

### ‚öôÔ∏è Conditions for Self-Control

Juran said employees must have:

1.  **Knowledge of what to do** ‚Äî clear goals, standards, and expectations.  
    _Example:_ budgeted profit, delivery schedule, product specs.
2.  **Knowledge of how they are performing** ‚Äî feedback and metrics.  
    _Example:_ actual profit, delivery rate, defect rate.
3.  **Means to regulate performance** ‚Äî authority and tools to correct issues.  
    _Example:_ adjust the process or their own actions.

* * *

### üí° Purpose

To create **ownership** and **accountability**, enabling people to:

*   Detect and correct their own deviations from standards.
*   Maintain process stability and improvement.
*   Reduce dependence on supervisors and external control.

* * *

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Note

Juran cautioned that empowerment must be **balanced** ‚Äî giving people both authority **and** the ability (skills, tools, data) to control outcomes responsibly.

*   Changes **multiple factors simultaneously** according to a structured plan (factorial, fractional factorial, RSM, etc.).
*   Uses statistical balance (**orthogonality**) so all effects can be estimated cleanly.

### ‚úÖ Advantages

*   Reveals **interactions** between variables.
*   **Fewer runs** for the same information.
*   **Mathematically rigorous** ‚Äî supports hypothesis tests, modelling, and optimisation.

### ‚ùå Disadvantages

*   Requires planning, statistical knowledge, and software for analysis.

| Typical Use | Simple, early lab tests | Structured process optimisation |

### üí° In short

> OFAT = exploratory tinkering.  
> DOE = structured, statistically optimised experimentation.
